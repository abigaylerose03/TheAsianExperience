The Asian-American Women Collective

Welcome to the The Asian-American Collective!

Context: The Asian-American Women Collective community-oriented website that aims to mobilize and visualize the empowerment of Asian-American women from a holistic sensory-based viewpoint

Signifcant questions: How can we create safe communities offline and online? Despite Asians having more than half the world's population, why are they portrayed so negatively in the media

Stakes: This exploration matters because of the recurring negative portrayal of Asian women in American society. From persistent objectification to fetishnization in modern day media, my exploration proposes a solution to flip the script. Rather than reinforcing negative stereotypes, The Asian-American Women Collective celebrates our differences and bridges the cultural divisiveness people living in America perpetuate.

Your engagement with at least 2 texts from this class: Things People Said by Sejal Shah, Crying in H Mart by Michelle Zauner, & Monsoon and Peacock by Aimee Nezhukumatathil

Padma Lakshmi is an author, actor, model, television host and humanitarian. While she may be most known for hosting Bravo’s Top Chef, the Indian born celeb is also a UN Goodwill Ambassador. Lakshmi has been outspoken in her fight for equality having said many nations have greatly reduced poverty, but inequality compounded by gender, age, ethnicity and race has proved more stubborn.
Ali Wong is an American comedian, writer and actress; she is a household name, known for numerous Netflix specials, stand-up tours and her book, Dear Girls. Wong is known for drawing from her experiences as the child of Asian immigrants but also renouncing the idea that that is her defining quality. She regularly turns the stereotype of the overachieving and docile Asian woman on its head; Wong is leading the charge in the telling of the modern Asian American story.
Meet Sandy, entrepreneur by day and TikToker by night. As an immigrant from Taiwan, she came to the U.S to learn about how to start her own business. Sandy is the Founder of WROOOF, an e-commerce boutique specializing in pet accessories. She also created Small Business Tips, a community that empowers small business owners, helps dreamers become doers, and connects founders with external opportunities. At 22, Sandy has two well-established and successful ventures, so we sat down with her to learn more about her startup journey and how she managed to do it all. From a young age, Live Tinted founder Deepica Mutyala wanted to change the narrative within Indian culture that lighter skin is seen as more beautiful. Live Tinted started off as a digital community, where people from all different backgrounds and ethnicities came together to share their experiences with beauty. It was during that time she figured out what her first Live Tinted product would be: color-correcting crayons called Huesticks. While Huestick won a Best of Beauty Award last year, we're also quite the fans of the dewy, highlighter-like Hueglow and hydrating Unity Balm Gloss.
