The Asian-American Women Collective

Welcome to the The Asian-American Collective!

Context: The Asian-American Women Collective community-oriented website that aims to mobilize and visualize the empowerment of Asian-American women from a holistic sensory-based viewpoint

Signifcant questions: How can we create safe communities offline and online? Despite Asians having more than half the world's population, why are they portrayed so negatively in the media

Stakes: This exploration matters because of the recurring negative portrayal of Asian women in American society. From persistent objectification to fetishnization in modern day media, my exploration proposes a solution to flip the script. Rather than reinforcing negative stereotypes, The Asian-American Women Collective celebrates our differences and bridges the cultural divisiveness people living in America perpetuate.

Your engagement with at least 2 texts from this class: Things People Said by Sejal Shah, Crying in H Mart by Michelle Zauner, & Monsoon and Peacock by Aimee Nezhukumatathil

Ali Wong is a well-known American comedian, writer, and actor, best known for her Netflix specials, stand-up tours, and book, Dear Girls. Wong is renowned for sharing her experiences as the child of Asian immigrants, although she rejects the notion that this is her defining characteristic.
